Academic Profile

As a scholar-practitioner with over two decades of experience in emergent media, I explore how immersive learning environments, Extended Reality technologies, game-based learning strategies, and artificial intelligence can transform educational experiences and creative practice. My PhD in Learning Technologies from the University of North Texas, combined with experience at institutions including Microsoft, ACU, and SMU Guildhall, positions me to bridge creative practice with pedagogical theory in emergent media education.

Currently serving as Chair of the Department of Integrated Business Technologies, Communication, and Marketing at Lubbock Christian University, I have built and led innovative programs in Digital Media Applications, Information Systems & Technology, and Game Design. I developed LCU's Varsity Esports Program and now serve as its Executive Director, integrating competitive gaming with academic learning and professional development. My research agenda explores the convergence of immersive technologies, artificial intelligence applications in education, and experiential learning‚Äîdirectly addressing questions central to the future of animation, game design, and emergent media pedagogy.

Research Vision & Impact

My research agenda centers on understanding how emergent media technologies‚Äîparticularly immersive XR environments, AI-driven systems, and game-based frameworks‚Äîcan transform learning, creativity, and human interaction. This work is fundamentally interdisciplinary, drawing on learning sciences, game studies, human-computer interaction, and media theory to address both pedagogical questions and broader implications of technological innovation.

Through my metaversity research (recognized with multiple best paper awards), I investigate how persistent virtual environments and XR technologies create new possibilities for synchronous engagement, spatial learning, and embodied interaction. My work on the Persona AI Model explores theoretical frameworks for understanding AI consciousness and human-AI collaboration, while my research on learner typologies bridges game player theory with online education to better understand diverse learner needs and behaviors.

Beyond scholarly publication, my research directly informs curriculum design and pedagogical practice. I have created entirely new courses in immersive worlds, game design, and disruptive technologies; developed innovative assessment strategies that bridge academic and professional contexts; and led faculty development initiatives on AI integration and online engagement. This scholar-practitioner approach ensures that theoretical insights translate into tangible improvements in how students learn, create, and prepare for careers in rapidly evolving media landscapes.
