I developed LCU's Varsity Esports Program and now serve as its Executive Director, integrating competitive gaming with academic learning and professional development. Winter Survival VR Simulation

The Winter Survival Exercise is an immersive VR simulation developed to examine student engagement in synchronous online learning through the metaversity concept. Students enter a virtual environment as avatars where they must work collaboratively to survive a plane crash in a remote winter location. The exercise requires teams to prioritize 12 survival items, demonstrating how diverse teams perform better on complex tasks than individuals working alone. Built using Unity game engine and Massive Loop platform, the project integrates management education principlesâ€”including team dynamics, decision-making under pressure, and resource prioritizationâ€”within an experiential XR environment. This research won Best Paper Award at the Academy of Business Research Conference (2024) and Best in Track at Southwest Academy of Management (2025), with findings published in Organizational Management Journal examining whether students perceive higher engagement levels in immersive learning environments compared to traditional online formats.

Immersive Extended Reality, the Metaversity, and the Future of Online Higher Education
In Press: Organizational Management Journal
Kucinski, N., Burton, B., Darby, D., Hamm, S., Tanner, R. & McKell, C.

Research examining XR technologies and metaverse concepts for higher education. Winner of Best Paper Award at Academy of Business Research Conference (2024) and Best in Track Award at Southwest Academy of Management Conference (2025).

Purpose: This paper discusses the potential for developing an educational campus in the metaverse, provides a demonstration of an immersive virtual learning environment, and presents a description of the interdisciplinary collaboration focused on incorporating immersive serious game development in online education.

Design/methodology/approach: The paper presents a description of a prototype immersive virtual reality environment that was created using Unity Game Engine (v.2022.3), with Massive Loop handling multiplayer functionality. Due to technical instability in the immersive platform, students in an online organizational behavior course did not ultimately participate in the immersive environment; instead, students were surveyed regarding their perceptions of engagement during synchronous online class sessions that incorporated interactive and simulation-based activities.

Findings: The prototype immersive virtual reality learning environment demonstrates the potential for immersive serious games within a Metaversity context. Survey results indicate that students perceive synchronous online sessions incorporating interactive and simulation-based activities as engaging and valuable, suggesting design implications for future immersive learning environments.

Originality/value: This paper further develops the literature of online learning and student engagement by illustrating the impacts of immersive virtual reality as an online learning tool. In addition, the interdisciplinary approach to developing the prototype environment expands, not only the application of technology, but also demonstrates the quality of serious game development for use in the metaverse that goes beyond standard off-the-shelf immersive environments for online educational purposes.

Under Review

An Online Learner Typology Informed by Game-Based Engagement and Pedagogical Frameworks
Under Review: Educational Technology Research and Development
Darby, D. & Warren, S. J.
Over the past two decades, researchers have explored parallels between online gaming environments and digital learning systems. Both domains leverage virtual contexts and pedagogical dynamics that foster participant interaction and experiential engagement to build the skills and knowledge required for accomplishing targeted goals. The diverse mechanics, interactive structures, and constructive objectives of games indicate their function as complex systems for skill and knowledge development. Games offer educators a meaningful representation of digitally enhanced learning and illustrate effective interactive instructional strategies. This review examines theoretical models from game studies and online pedagogy to assess their relevance for defining individual learner characteristics in virtual learning contexts. Key constructs related to play behavior, motivation, and user engagement are synthesized to assess their applicability to a new typology of online learners. The findings reveal substantial theoretical overlap among game-based engagement models and learner diversity in digital instructional environments, providing a conceptual foundation for future taxonomies.

The Persona Model: A Framework for Tacit Knowledge Reconstruction and Contextual AI Interaction
In Preparation
Darby, D. & Anderson, G.

Novel framework for AI-based knowledge constructs and human-computer interaction environments. EquipLCU Research Grant recipient ($1,000).

The rapid adoption of generative artificial intelligence has intensified long-standing challenges related to the preservation, transfer, and ethical use of tacit knowledge. While contemporary AI systems excel at pattern recognition and content generation, they remain largely disconnected from the contextual, experiential, and value-laden dimensions of human cognition that underpin expertise, judgment, and organizational memory. This paper introduces the Persona Model, a conceptual framework designed to reconstruct and sustain tacit knowledge through ethically bounded, context-aware AI interaction. The model conceptualizes AI not as a replacement for human expertise, but as a collaborative cognitive partner that preserves identity, intent, and epistemic continuity over time. Drawing from theories of tacit knowledge, organizational learning, humanâ€“AI collaboration, and ethical AI design, the Persona Model integrates modular architectural components that govern knowledge intake, contextual reasoning, moral constraint, and adaptive interaction. The framework emphasizes continuity rather than accumulation, enabling AI systems to evolve alongside their source contexts while maintaining fidelity to originating values and cognitive structures. This paper articulates the theoretical foundations, structural architecture, and intended applications of the Persona Model, positioning it as a scalable approach for responsible AI deployment in educational, organizational, and knowledge-intensive domains. The model offers a pathway toward AI systems that support long-term human understanding rather than transient automation.
Bridging the AI Literacy Gap: Developing Ethical and Effective AI Education
In Preparation
Darby, D. & Harris, D.
Artificial Intelligence (AI) is rapidly reshaping industries and professions, yet higher education has not kept pace in preparing students to understand and use AI responsibly. The gap between the widespread adoption of AI tools and the limited awareness of their capabilities, limitations, and ethical implications presents a significant challenge for undergraduate education. The pedagogical model for interdisciplinary AI literacy education presented in this work is being developed through the design of the undergraduate course Ethical and Effective Use of AI. This course is intended to equip students from diverse academic backgrounds with the knowledge and skills necessary to engage productively and ethically with AI.

The model follows a scaffolded structure, moving from foundational understanding of AI concepts to advanced applications. Core elements include prompt development, critical evaluation of AI-generated outputs, collaborative interaction with AI systems, and the examination of ethical considerations across multiple domains. Learning activities incorporate scenario-based challenges, case study analysis, and peer-reviewed projects that require both technical practice and critical reflection. Formative assessments and applied projects are used to promote skill development, transference of knowledge, and long-term understanding.

By positioning AI literacy as both a technical and ethical imperative, the model addresses the accelerating need for higher education to prepare students for emerging workforce and societal demands. Drawing lessons from past disruptive innovations, such as the rapid emergence of the Internet and the complexities of digital intellectual property, it emphasizes the importance of proactive education to mitigate misuse, overreliance, and displacement concerns. Its interdisciplinary orientation makes it adaptable to a wide range of academic contexts, including business, communication, and technology, while also supporting students in the liberal arts.

This pedagogical model contributes to the broader conversation on AI in education by offering a replicable framework that emphasizes ethical engagement, collaborative interaction, and effective utilization of AI tools. It provides educators and curriculum designers with a practical foundation for integrating AI literacy into undergraduate programs and preparing students for an evolving professional and academic landscape.
A Framework for Ethical and Effective Humanâ€“AI Engagement
In Preparation
Harris, D. & Darby, D.
As artificial intelligence systems become increasingly embedded in educational, organizational, and decision-making contexts, questions of ethical use, cognitive alignment, and effective engagement have moved beyond technical performance toward human-centered concerns. Existing approaches to AI governance often emphasize control, compliance, or post-hoc mitigation, offering limited guidance for how humans should meaningfully and responsibly engage with AI systems in practice. This paper proposes a conceptual framework for ethical and effective humanâ€“AI engagement that foregrounds agency, transparency, contextual awareness, and relational responsibility. Drawing from humanâ€“computer interaction, learning theory, organizational ethics, and emerging AI ethics scholarship, the framework articulates key dimensions that shape productive humanâ€“AI relationships, including intent alignment, cognitive scaffolding, ethical boundaries, and feedback-driven adaptation. Rather than positioning AI as an autonomous authority or passive tool, the framework conceptualizes engagement as a dynamic partnership in which human judgment, values, and accountability remain central. The paper outlines the framework's core principles, structural components, and potential applications across educational, professional, and knowledge-intensive domains. By shifting the focus from AI control to engagement quality, this framework offers a foundation for designing and evaluating AI systems that support responsible use, sustained trust, and meaningful human outcomes.
Ethical Perceptions and Impacts of Generative AI in Faith-Based Higher Education
In Preparation (IRB Approved Exempt)
Darby, D., Hudson, K., & Bates, L.
Generative Artificial Intelligence (GAI) is transforming education, but its ethical implications are particularly complex in faith-based higher education institutions, where technology must align with deeply held belief-centric values and mission-driven goals. This study explores the ethical challenges of GAI adoption in faith-based settings, specifically focusing on conflicts and incompatibilities between AI-generated content and core institutional values and beliefs, potential biases within AI systems, and the alignment of policies and perceptions regarding the integration of technology into traditional educational models with the requirements of paradigms that prioritize moral and spiritual formation. While both secular and faith-based institutions share a consideration of institutional efficiency, educational outcomes, and basic ethical factors, faith-based institutions must also navigate the intersection of spiritually-oriented moral convictions and guiding principles with pedagogical considerations. The research hypothesizes that GAI presents distinct ethical challenges in these environments, shaping stakeholder perceptions and influencing decision-making processes. To investigate these challenges, a survey of 1,858 participants, including students, faculty, and administrators at Lubbock Christian University, was conducted, followed by planned qualitative interviews for deeper insights. Initial data collection indicates that stakeholders may recognize the potential of GAI to enhance educational innovation, but there are anticipated concerns regarding its alignment with faith-based values and the risk of bias in AI-generated content. Ongoing analysis will explore these expectations and test the reliability and validity of the survey instrument before extending the study to a broader range of faith-based higher education institutions. This study employs a mixed-methods approach, combining quantitative survey data with qualitative contextual interview response data in the analysis, to provide a comprehensive understanding of how faith-based institutions can integrate GAI solutions ethically without compromising their faith-integration missions. The findings are expected to contribute to the broader discourse on AI ethics, offering valuable insights for AI developers, educators, and policymakers on navigating the unique ethical considerations faced by faith-based educational institutions as they adopt GAI technologies.
Broadening Experiential Professional Development Constructs through the Metaverse
In Preparation
Darby, D. & Burton, B.
The world has experienced a dramatic shift over the last several years resulting in a change in how people communicate and learn. In this paper, the authors seek to provide a roadmap to the future of how training and professional development will transition in the near future into the immersive reality of the Metaverse and gain a conceptual understanding of the significant dynamics as it relates to training and professional development. To address deficits observed with existing professional development and training delivery strategies, the application of more constructive and experiential methods and modalities are needed. These approaches find meaningful manifestation for participants using immersive learning. This presentation examines the potential of the Metaverse to facilitate new strategies that offer a broader approach to facilitating the type of consistent learning flow needed for deeper levels of engagement and interaction while fostering meaningful, relevant outcomes at each level of professional development, particularly within the context of information systems technology. Using key strategies, such as online simulations, gamified learning, immersive environments, and other digital interactive instructional opportunities that broaden sensory and participatory engagement, organizations can leverage the principles of experiential learning to foster understanding and knowledge transference.

Research Vision & Impact

My research agenda centers on understanding how emergent media technologiesâ€”particularly immersive XR environments, AI-driven systems, and game-based frameworksâ€”can transform learning, creativity, and human interaction.
